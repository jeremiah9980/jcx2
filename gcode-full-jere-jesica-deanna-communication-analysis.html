6. The Core Communication Problem

What works

  • One child-specific topic at a time.
  • Doctor notes, prescriptions, pickup/dropoff, school attendance, and activity logistics.
  • Short confirmations: received, uploaded, picked up, on the way, done.
  • Jesica helping with logistics when her role is clear.
  • Deanna giving necessary school/medical/activity context when it stays factual.
  • Jere giving formal notice and schedule clarity without turning every message into a fight.

What breaks it

  • Combining money, children’s emotions, school stability, team reputation, legal issues, and Jesica’s role in one thread.
  • Statements that imply children should carry adult messages or understand adult blame.
  • Dragging coaches, relatives, team parents, or outside family into co-parent disputes.
  • Instructions to delete texts or hide child/legal-related communication.
  • Using softball as the public stage for parental role conflict.
  • Late communication that forces emergency-mode problem solving.
GCODE bottom line: The thread does not need more emotion. It needs lane discipline. Medical lane. School lane. Sports lane. Expenses lane. Possession lane. Legal lane. Keep them separate or the same fight keeps reproducing.
