Observed Pattern Summary

Boundaries trigger escalation

Triangulation

Financial leverage

Summary

The overall documented pattern is not one of constant hostility but of intermittent escalation. During those escalation periods, Sharon is described as moving from supportive or practical communication into intrusive questioning, guilt-based pressure, triangulation, unsupported accusations, and withdrawal of support. From a systems perspective, repeated third-party involvement is associated with increased triangulation, expanded conflict framing, and reduced ability to maintain narrow, child-focused communication.
