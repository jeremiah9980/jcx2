Seven Documented Operating Tactics

1. Child as Messenger and Emotional Weapon

A child’s reported feelings, preferences, fear, crying, or therapy needs are repeatedly used as evidence in adult disputes. The concern is that a child becomes both messenger and leverage.

2. Financial Coercion Linked to Child Welfare

Financial issues are framed as direct harm to the children, such as eviction, school changes, loss of room, or lack of Christmas. This removes middle ground and turns a financial issue into a parental-worth verdict.

3. Third-Party Coordination

4. Public Narrative Seeding

Grievances are described as being broadcast to coaches, team parents, extended family, or other community audiences before facts are established.

5. Chaos as Control / Topic Stacking

Large multi-topic messages combine finances, children, third parties, legal claims, therapy, court history, sports, and character accusations. This makes any clean response difficult.

6. Intermittent Reinforcement

Cooperative and warm windows occur between escalation periods. The pattern is not constant hostility; it is escalation, softening, baseline, and new trigger.

7. Retroactive Narrative Construction

Unrelated or parallel issue tracks are later merged into a single continuous narrative of wrongdoing. The March 5 screenshot dump is identified as an example.

Behavioral Profile Summary

The source record describes a consistent operating structure: establish moral authority, act unilaterally, deploy children as evidence, consolidate the narrative, briefly cooperate, and repeat.

The pattern is not described as random or constant hostility. It is cyclic, with cooperative windows that are real but temporary. The strongest documentation is identified in the Kassidy ARD and school-related records because those materials involve independent institutional data.

For practical response strategy, the key lesson is not merely understanding Deanna’s pattern, but recognizing when the pattern activates in real time and choosing responses that do not feed it. Accurate legalistic rebuttals may be defensible but can also extend exchanges and provide more material for conflict.
